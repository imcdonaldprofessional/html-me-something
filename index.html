What are you talking about?

Back in July 2020, a game called Internet League Blaseball, or just "Blaseball," went live. A play on the popular sport, Blaseball is a weird, fantastical version of Baseball, although most Blaseball fans will pretend not to know what you're talking about when you mention Baseball. If you log on to blaseball.com, depending on what day of the week it is, all you'll see is twenty teams, constantly playing games every hour, on the hour.

How do you play?

Well, you don't play Blaseball, so much as you participate in it. Mechanically, whenever you sign up, you select a favorite team. Whenever your team wins a game, you're gifted a small amount of "coins." You can use these coins to place bets on games, earning more money and being able to place larger bets as time goes on.

You might be asking yourself, "why do I care how much money I make bidding on this fake internet sports thing?" One of the things that makes Blaseball so interesting is that at the end of each season, fans are allowed to use their money to purchase votes, just like in real life. These votes can be cast towards "Blessings" that improve various elements of their favorite team. Initially, these were very straightforward, but as time has gone on, things have gotten a bit more complicated.

Okay then, how do I "participate?"

So, first thing you want to do is join a team! There's no real trick to picking your favorite. Some folks pick the team closest to their hometown, others just pick one that sounds fun. Each team has a different personality informed by lots of small details.

For example, the Baltimore Crabs have a very active fanbase. As a vocal, organized group, they were able to push harder than most teams for coordination in post-season voting. These tactics paid off in securing the Crabs lots of Blessings and made them the first team to win three championships, but combined with their overactive fans, it also characterized them as "heels," the villains of Blaseball.

What's cool about this anyway?

That's a very fair question! Obviously the actual interface for Blaseball is pretty minimalist, but the fan community on Discord, a chat room service, is extremely active.

People are constantly creating 'lore' for Blaseball events or characters, some fans even run twitter accounts dedicated to roleplaying a specific Blaseball character.
